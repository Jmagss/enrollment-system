Enrolment System of Mandaue City College


Project Context


The present system at the school, such as the enrollment system and record keeping, is entirely manual. The manual system has been in existence since the school first opened its doors. Students will visit the admissions office throughout the enrollment period to obtain any necessary enrollment requirements. The evaluator will then look at the pupils' grades to see which topics they should study this school year and how they are doing academically. The assessor will come up with a list of subjects that the pupils must take. After getting the list, students will pay the cashier their PTA fee and other fees.The cashier will issue receipts to the students as proof that they have already paid. At the end of the enrollment period, the person in charge will prepare a summary of student enrolment.

Due to disorganized student data, tallying subjects takes time as well. The manual updating of the student's records slows down the admission process since the enrolling officer must seek and locate the student's records in the filing cabinet. Student evaluations are delayed due to the enrolling officer's need to check previous grades and the fact that some of the student's records have gone missing. Tallying subjects takes time as well, due to unstructured student data.


Due to these considerations, the researchers decided to design and build a computerized enrollment system that is quick, accurate, dependable, and easy to use. The institution's proposed enrollment process is a positive step in the right direction. This might be a tremendous help to folks who are juggling a lot of tasks during the registration period by providing quick and easy access to information. The main purpose of this capstone project is to develop a computer-based enrollment system that is fast, accurate, dependable, and easy to use.For the School's management, professors, staff, and students, the researchers will design and build a capstone project titled "Computerized Enrollment System." The proposed project will use computer-based software to replace the school's present manual enrollment system, which will allow authorized users to speed up the enrolling process, provide updated, secure, and easy-to-access student records, and provide accurate reports.


The proposed project will replace the school's current manual enrollment system with computer-based software that will allow authorized users to speed up the enrolment process, provide updated, secure, and easy-to-access student records, and generate accurate reports. The user's log report, list of enrolled students, enrollment summary reports, payments and collectibles, and student ledger are all possible outputs from the proposed project.Admission officers can easily update student records, evaluate students, provide a list of student subjects, record payments, provide a list of collectibles, and manage credentials such as Form 137, among other benefits provided by the proposed project for school administration, office staff, and students.This project will offer the school with an enrolment database. It will keep track of student information such as name, year, section, timetable, and school fees. It might also be used by the school's finance department to pay for school fees. The database is intended for use by registrar office personnel and other authorized users to enable them to quickly produce information needed by various people in the institution. Enrollment and fee collection for the school will be simple and quick thanks to the database system.
